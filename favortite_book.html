The Maze Runner

By James Dashner

The Maze Runner is a book series where the world is ravaged by sun flares that grew too large and ended up wiping half the world clean into wasteland and radiation. Then a little down the line the government (I think it's supposed to be the American government.) decides to infect small designated areas with a virus that was supposed to help with population control, where then the virus evolves in a way that nobody expected resulting in a situation very similar to a zombie apocalypse.

The main character of the story is definitely a boy named Thomas who was one of many children of the next generation called "The Immune" who, as the name suggests; they are immune to the virus that plagues the world named The Flare. Thomas along with a multitude of other "Immune" are essentially kidnapped for the purpose of finding a cure, no matter the cost.

Genre:
Dystopian/Sci-Fi
Rating:
I would rate this book a solid 4.8/5 stars. The reason being I have almost no interest in books though, this one was unique. I really enjoyed the story line and couldn't really predict anything. This book was full of surprises and I would highly recommend this book to anyone who enjoys the idea of a "zombie apocalypse".
Something I took away from this book:
There is such a thing as an entertaining book series.
